Learn Web Development
Mozilla Developer Network

Mozilla Developer Network

This site will provide loads of documentation of web development. Its goal is to get you “comfortable” with development, though placing an unusual emphasis on why things work. There are activities throughout the documents, particularly in the higher level ones. However, a warning must go out to those who don’t have tons of time, as this certainly requires massive amounts of that. One page takes about 30-45 minutes to complete. It’s also highly recommended you take notes, while reading the documents

Pros

  • In depth explanations
  • Interactive activities
  • Logical progression of concepts
  • Miniature, optional, exams for every module

Upon completion, you should feel comfortable with web development, while not necessarily being a professional

Cons

  • Extremely time consuming
  • Pretty boring
  • Limited specifics
  • No frameworks explained

Those with limited patience will not appreciate this resource

W3Schools

W3Schools

Very quick bites of highly specific information are easily found in the logically laid out library. Think of this more as a Google search for web development concepts, than a guided tutorial. However, the freedom offers great flexibility for developing your own site. Most people will migrate to this one after they’ve got the basic concepts mastered. It’s questionably useful for complete beginners

Pros

  • Quick explanation of specific tags
  • Easy to navigate library
  • Great explanations advanced development
  • Best glossary or reference

Has every single piece of information on every element, property, ect. Always up to date with information

Cons

  • Limited explanation of why things work
  • No clear relation between concepts
  • Poor explanation for complete beginners

It’s likely you may still feel uncomfortable with development, after completing this course

Codecademy

Codecademy

It’s quite likely you’ve heard about Codecademy in you past searches, as the majority of people seem to learn web development off here. This one does teach you to code through making you code, which theoretically should simulate a real-life development situation. However, the hand-held guided approach will frustrate many of those who would like to develop with their own style. Such a service will only provide the fundamentals of developing sites

Pros

  • Code within the lesson site
  • Live updates on code
  • Notices and points out syntax errors
  • Very guided practice

You will learn to code by actually coding and not just learning theory

Cons

  • Highly restrictive environment
  • No in depth explanation of concepts
  • Extra features cost money
  • Poor simulation of real development

Ends up giving many beginners a false sense of what web development actually consists of, by completely cutting out the creativity in web design

Freecodecamp

Freecodecamp

A very similar site to Codecademy. In differences, you’ll find this one has a lot more prominence in how to build the site most efficiently. While this may sound good, beginners usually have the goal of learning not developing, which requires a different mindset. Efficiency is only valued for professionals, who don’t care how it gets done, as long as it gets done. Those who already mastered basic concepts commonly go back to this one, to improve their efficiency

Pros

  • Live view is like a phone screen
  • Hundreds of hours of free content
  • See Codecademy (above) for other pros

Phone screen simulates more likely scenario. It teaches the most efficient way to develop sites

Cons

  • Extremely restrictive
  • Too much emphasis on frameworks
  • Lacks emphasis on custom CSS

Being even more restrictive than Codecademy seriously leaves the wrong impression on beginners. It focuses more on building the site than learning to build a site

Sololearn

Sololearn

With a large community and multiple social media-like concepts, Sololearn keeps you in touch will people in all stages of learning. The actual learning area is very self motivated, hence the name Sololearn. It provides an uncontested mobile experience, which is great for those on the go. Even the method of splitting content feels like it was made for people who only have 20 minutes a day, for quick learning sessions. Highly recommended on public transit

Pros

  • Bite size content for those with limited time
  • In depth explanation of concepts
  • Built in interpreter for most languages
  • Massive community
  • Daily challenges with limited restrictions
  • Great for mobile devices

Gives you a great look at basic concepts, while taking hardly any time. Community leaderboards show others’ real world application of concepts

Cons

  • Unusual way of examining concepts
  • Limited incentive for completion
  • No real world application of concepts

Unlike most others, this one seriously requires heavy self motivation to complete
